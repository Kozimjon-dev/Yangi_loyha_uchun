The History of Tea

Paragraph A: Tea is the second most consumed drink in the world, surpassed only by water. An often-told story says that in 2737 BC, the Chinese Emperor Shen Nung was sitting under a tree when some leaves blew into his boiling water. He decided to try it, and that was how tea was discovered.

Paragraph B: Tea was first introduced to Portuguese priests and merchants in China during the 16th century. Drinking tea became fashionable in Britain during the 17th century. The British introduced tea production, as well as tea consumption, to India, in order to compete with the Chinese monopoly on tea.

Paragraph C: Today, there are many different types of tea. Black tea is the most common in the West, while green tea is preferred in East Asia. Each type of tea requires different water temperatures and steeping times to get the best flavor.
